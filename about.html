About Me

I grew up in the suburbs of Atlanta, GA. There, I attended Kennesaw State University after completing my general education requirements at a community college in the area. In December of 2020, I graduated from KSU with a B.S. in Information Technolgy with a concentration in Mobile & Web Development. After graduation, I relocated to Seattle, WA, to pursue a career in the field I love.

My love of technology sprouted soon after starting high school, but I found a home in web development in 2012. After joining a popular blogging platform, I discovered a community of users developing custom themes for their blogs. These users inspired me to learn how to create my own custom themes. I began studying HTML, CSS, vanilla JavaScript, and jQuery between classes, and began contributing to the coding community on the site soon after. Within three years, I had shared approximately 25 projects on the platform.

By my junior year of college, I had taken a hiatus from posting on my blog in order to dedicate more time to my studies. In that time, I refined my design style and settled on somewhat of a brand identity. I have yet to return to posting on the site, but I continue to follow and interact with the community that started it all for me. I plan to revisit my previous work; updating what I feel I can improve on and archiving what I no longer identify with as a developer.

When I'm not working on web development, I can be found gaming, listening to music, or completing various D.I.Y. home improvement projects.

I pride myself on always being true to my word, genuine, and ambitious.
